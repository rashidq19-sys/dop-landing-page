Section 8 — A Morning With DSPOps (Storyboard)
See it in action

A morning with DSPOps

It's 6:00 AM. Dispatch starts. Here's how DSPOps keeps everything running smoothly.

6:00

OSM opens the dashboard

Today's rota is already set. Drivers assigned to routes. Van allocations done. No spreadsheet needed.

6:10

Attendance check — live

Drivers check in on the app. The OSM sees who's arrived and who's missing — no clipboard, no guessing.

6:15

⚠️ Someone doesn't show up

A driver is missing. The OSM taps their name, calls them. Not coming? One tap to find an available replacement and reassign the route. Done.

6:20

Keys handed over, vans tracked

Each van handover is logged. The OSM sees exactly which vans are out and which are still waiting.

6:25

Van photos uploaded — instantly visible

Drivers upload pre-departure van photos and videos. The OSM sees them in real time — and can immediately spot who hasn't uploaded yet.

6:30

✅ Dispatch complete. Everyone's on the road.

30 minutes. No spreadsheets, no chaos, no shouting across the yard. The owner checks the dashboard from home — everything's green.

That's dispatch done in 30 minutes. Your OSM can now focus on improving performance — not firefighting.

Design notes:

  • How It Works: Kept simple — 3 steps, same as current but slightly reworded. This is the quick overview.
  • Morning storyboard: Dark background to feel immersive and different from the rest of the page — like "stepping into the morning"
  • Timeline format: Each step has a timestamp (6:00-6:30 AM), making it feel real and concrete
  • The "problem" moment (6:15): Red-tinted card when someone doesn't show up — shows the app handles the chaos, not the OSM
  • The payoff (6:30): Green — "dispatch complete, owner checks from home" — reinforces both OSM and owner value
  • Closing line: Ties back to the big promise — OSM focuses on performance, not firefighting
